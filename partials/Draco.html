Fun Facts about Draco:

  • It is located between Ursa Major and Ursa Minor in the northern sky
  • It was first named as a constellation by 2nd-century astronomer Ptolemy
  • The north pole of the sun's path is in Draco
  • It is circumpolar, meaning it is "never setting"
  • It can be seen all year from northern latitudes
  • In Greek and Roman mythology, Draco was a dragon killed by the goddess Minerva and tossed into the sky upon his defeat
