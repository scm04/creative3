Fun Facts about Ursa Major:

  • It is one of the constellations in the northern sky
  • It is more commonly known as the Big Dipper in North America
  • Ursa Major is Latin meaning "the greater (or larger) she-bear"
  • It was first named as a constellation by 2nd-century astronomer Ptolemy
  • Its name is a reference to Ursa Minor
  • The two Ursa constellations were named because of their frequent association in mythology
  • The two stars on the outer edge of the Big Dipper's bowl can be used to find Polaris
  • Ursa Major is the third-largest constellation in the sky
