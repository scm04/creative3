Fun Facts about Cassiopeia:

  • It is one of the constellations in the northern sky
  • It was first named as a constellation by 2nd-century astronomer Ptolemy
  • It is named after the vain queen Cassiopeia, from Greek mythology
  • It is easily recognizable due to its distinctive "W" shape
  • In Greek mythology, Cassiopeia was the wife of King Cepheus of Aethiopia and the mother of princess Andromeda (both of whom also have constellations named after them)
  • According to the myths, Cassiopeia was placed in the sky as punishment for angering Poseidon
  • It is the 25th-largest constellation
  • It is circumpolar (always visible) to viewers in the British Isles, Canada, and the northern United States
