<p>Fun Facts about Ursa Minor:</p>
<ul>
<li>It is one of the constellations in the northern sky</li>
<li>It is more commonly known as the Little Dipper in North America</li>
<li>Ursa Minor is Latin meaning "the lesser she-bear"</li>
<li>The tail of the constellation contains Polaris (the North Star)</li>
<li>It was first named as a constellation by 2nd-century astronomer Ptolemy</li>
<li>Because it contains Polaris, Ursa Minor has traditionally been important for navigation</li>
<li>It is mentioned by different names in various texts as early as 1000 BC</li>
<li>The Greeks related Ursas Major and Minor to the myth of Callisto and her son Arcas</li>
</ul>
