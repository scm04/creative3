Fun Facts about Ursa Minor:
-It is one of the constellations in the northern sky
-It is more commonly known as the Little Dipper in North America
-Ursa Minor is Latin meaning "the lesser she-bear"
-The tail of the constellation contains Polaris (the North Star)
-It was first named as a constellation by 2nd-century astronomer Ptolemy
-Because it contains Polaris, Ursa Minor has traditionally been important for navigation
-It is mentioned by different names in various texts as early as 1000 BC
-The Greeks related Ursas Major and Minor to the myth of Callisto and her son Arcas
